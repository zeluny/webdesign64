Penfolds (Australia)

Penfolds, founded in Adelaide in 1844, is one of Australia’s oldest wine producers. They follow three distinct wine production styles - single vineyard, single region, multi-region, and multi varietal blending. The predominant grape varieties are Shiraz, Cabernet Sauvignon, Pinot Noir, Pinot Gris, Chardonnay, and Riesling.

Penfolds Grange

Penfolds Grange is a multi-region, multi varietal blend and is widely regarded as a collectors' item. This Australian First Growth equivalent is made predominantly from Syrah grapes and a small percentage of Cabernet Sauvignon and is priced anywhere over $500. As for the Grange experience, a bottle of Bin 95 Grange Shiraz 2014 is spicy, intense, and has hints of oak and ripe tannin. As for the price, a Penfolds Grange will cost you anything above $500.

Penfolds Bin 311 Chardonnay

The Bin 311 Chardonnay uses fruit sourced from Adelaide Hills, Tasmania, and Tumbarumba. This wine offers mineral acidity, citrus flavors and complex aromas of crème brûlée, ripe white nectarine and peach fruit, much to the delight of any wine enthusiast.

The 2016 Yattarna Chardonnay with roasted almonds has stone fruit and vanilla flavors.
